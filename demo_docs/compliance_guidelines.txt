HIGHWATCH AI — DATA COMPLIANCE AND PRIVACY GUIDELINES
Version 1.2 | Classification: Internal | Effective: January 2025
================================================================

1. PURPOSE
-----------
This document outlines Highwatch AI's compliance obligations and internal
data privacy guidelines in accordance with applicable laws including India's
Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act 2023 and GDPR where applicable.

2. DATA WE COLLECT
-------------------
Customer Data:
- Name, email, phone number (for account creation)
- Payment information (processed via PCI-DSS compliant gateways only)
- Usage logs and interaction history with our AI systems

Employee Data:
- Full name, date of birth, address, emergency contacts
- Bank account details for payroll
- Performance reviews and disciplinary records
- Health-related information (for insurance and leave management)

3. HOW WE USE DATA
-------------------
- Customer data is used ONLY to deliver contracted services
- Data is never sold to third parties
- Analytics are performed on anonymized/aggregated data only
- AI model training uses only consented and anonymized datasets

4. DATA RETENTION POLICY
-------------------------
- Customer account data: Retained for 3 years after account closure
- Transaction records: 7 years (as per financial regulations)
- Employee records: 7 years after employment ends
- System logs: 90 days rolling
- Backup data: Encrypted, retained for 1 year

5. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS
-----------------------
All individuals whose data we hold have the right to:
- Access their personal data (respond within 30 days)
- Correct inaccurate data (respond within 15 days)
- Delete their data ("right to erasure") — applicable where legally permissible
- Withdraw consent for processing
- Port their data to another service provider

To exercise rights, contact: privacy@highwatch.ai

6. DATA BREACH RESPONSE PROCEDURE
-----------------------------------
In the event of a data breach:
Step 1: Identify and contain the breach immediately
Step 2: Notify CISO and Legal within 1 hour of discovery
Step 3: Assess the scope: what data, how many individuals, what risk?
Step 4: Notify affected individuals within 72 hours if high-risk breach
Step 5: Report to regulatory authority (as required by DPDP Act) within 72 hours
Step 6: Document the incident in the breach register
Step 7: Conduct root cause analysis and implement preventive measures

7. THIRD-PARTY DATA SHARING
-----------------------------
We may share data with:
- Cloud providers (AWS, GCP) — under Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)
- Payment gateways (Razorpay, Stripe) — PCI-DSS compliant
- HR software vendors — under strict confidentiality agreements

We do NOT share data with:
- Advertising networks
- Data brokers
- Government entities (except when legally compelled, with legal review)

8. EMPLOYEE COMPLIANCE OBLIGATIONS
------------------------------------
All employees must:
- Complete annual data privacy training (mandatory, tracked in HRMS)
- Report any suspected data breach immediately
- Not access customer data beyond their job function
- Sign the Data Confidentiality Agreement on joining
- Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination

9. COMPLIANCE CONTACTS
-----------------------
Data Protection Officer (DPO): dpo@highwatch.ai
Privacy Queries: privacy@highwatch.ai
Legal and Compliance: legal@highwatch.ai
Security Incidents: it-security@highwatch.ai

10. AUDIT AND REVIEW
---------------------
- Internal compliance audits are conducted every 6 months
- External audits by certified auditors are conducted annually
- Policy violations are logged and reviewed by the Compliance Committee
- This document is reviewed and updated annually or upon regulatory change
