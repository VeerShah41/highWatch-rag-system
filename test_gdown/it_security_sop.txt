HIGHWATCH AI — IT SECURITY STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP)
Version 1.4 | Classification: Internal | Effective: March 2025
================================================================

1. PURPOSE
-----------
This SOP defines the information security standards that all Highwatch AI
employees, contractors, and vendors must follow to protect company data,
systems, and intellectual property.

2. PASSWORD POLICY
-------------------
- Minimum password length: 12 characters
- Must include: uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters
- Password must be changed every 90 days
- Do NOT reuse the last 10 passwords
- Use the company-approved password manager: LastPass Enterprise
- Never share passwords via email, Slack, or any messaging tool
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is MANDATORY for all company accounts

3. DATA CLASSIFICATION
-----------------------
Level 1 — Public:
  - Marketing materials, published blog posts, press releases
  - Can be shared externally without approval

Level 2 — Internal:
  - Employee handbook, internal SOPs, meeting notes
  - Can be shared within the company; not externally

Level 3 — Confidential:
  - Client data, financial records, product roadmaps, source code
  - Requires manager approval to share; encryption required

Level 4 — Restricted:
  - Legal documents, M&A data, security credentials, PII
  - Access restricted to specific roles; sharing requires CISO approval

4. DEVICE SECURITY
-------------------
- All company laptops must have full-disk encryption enabled (FileVault/BitLocker)
- Automatic screen lock after 5 minutes of inactivity
- Anti-malware software must be installed and updated weekly
- Never connect to public WiFi without a VPN
- Report lost/stolen devices to it-security@highwatch.ai IMMEDIATELY
- Personal devices may not be used to access Level 3/4 data

5. ACCESS CONTROL
------------------
- Access to systems is granted on a need-to-know basis (Principle of Least Privilege)
- New employee access is provisioned within 48 hours of joining
- Access must be revoked within 24 hours of an employee's last working day
- Annual access reviews are conducted every December
- Privileged access (admin) requires written justification and CISO approval

6. INCIDENT REPORTING
----------------------
What counts as a security incident:
- Phishing emails received or clicked
- Unauthorized access to company systems
- Data leakage or accidental sharing of confidential info
- Lost or stolen devices
- Malware or ransomware detection

How to report:
1. Immediately notify it-security@highwatch.ai
2. Do NOT attempt to fix the issue yourself
3. Preserve all evidence (do not delete emails, logs)
4. Complete the Incident Report Form in the IT portal within 2 hours
5. Security team will respond within 1 hour during business hours

7. ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY
-------------------------
Employees MAY use company systems for:
- Performing job responsibilities
- Approved business communication
- Accessing authorized cloud tools (G Suite, Jira, Slack, GitHub)

Employees MAY NOT use company systems for:
- Personal social media or streaming
- Downloading pirated software or media
- Cryptocurrency mining
- Accessing competitor systems
- Any illegal activities

8. VENDOR AND THIRD-PARTY ACCESS
----------------------------------
- All vendors must sign the Vendor Security Agreement before access is granted
- Vendor access must be time-limited and logged
- Vendors must adhere to the same standards as employees
- Vendor access is reviewed quarterly

9. BACKUP AND RECOVERY
-----------------------
- All production data is backed up daily to encrypted cloud storage
- Backup retention: 90 days for daily, 1 year for monthly
- Disaster recovery drills are conducted every 6 months
- RTO (Recovery Time Objective): 4 hours
- RPO (Recovery Point Objective): 24 hours
